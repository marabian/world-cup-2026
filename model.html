Methodology & how this differs from the market report

How this differs from the first report. The earlier page anchored its probabilities to bookmaker lines. This one ignores betting markets entirely: I gave each of the 24 teams an attack & defence rating from raw scoring/conceding rates — correcting for schedule inflation (Belgium's 3.6 goals/game came vs UEFA minnows; Curaçao's 0.3 conceded was a CONCACAF artifact) — then simulated the full scoreline distribution. Most picks agree with the market, but the magnitudes differ: my model is more skeptical of Uruguay and more draw-leaning on Mexico–Korea.

  1. Raw inputs. For all 24 teams: goals scored/conceded per game and any available xG/xGA over the last ~10–15 competitive matches (2026 qualifiers + 2025–26 friendlies + World Cup Matchday 1), plus strength-of-schedule.
  2. Ratings. Each team gets atk (expected goals vs an average defence) and def (expected goals conceded vs an average attack), neutral pitch. Raw rates are corrected: blowout outliers trimmed, minnow-inflated numbers and weak-schedule clean sheets regressed toward WC-level means, key absences docked (Pulisic, N'Dicka, R. Araújo, Neymar/Rodrygo, Yamal).
  3. Expected goals per match. λ_home = atk_home × (def_away / 1.35) × venue, symmetrically for the away side. Hosts (Mexico, USA) get ×1.10, opponent ×0.97; all other games neutral.
  4. Scoreline distribution. A bivariate Poisson grid (0–10 each) with the Dixon-Coles low-score correction (ρ=-0.07) to fix pure Poisson's under-count of 0-0/1-0/0-1/1-1.
  5. Outputs. Win/draw/loss, both-teams-to-score, over/under 2.5 and the most-likely scoreline are read straight off that matrix — fully reproducible.
